EXAMPLE

Solve the following system of equations:

          2x1 + 3x2 - x3 = 5
          4x1 + 4x2 - 3x3 = 3
          -2x1 + 3x2 + 2x3 = -1

SOLUTION

Step 1: Form the Augmented Matrix

\[ [A|b] = \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\ 4 & 4 & -3 & 3 \\ -2 & 3 & 2 & -1 \end{array} \right] \]

Step 2: Forward Elimination

Row Operation 1

Subtract 2 times the first row from the second row:

\[ R_2 \rightarrow R_2 - 2R_1 \]

Resulting matrix:

\[ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & -1 & -7 \\ -2 & 3 & 2 & -1 \end{array} \right] \]

Row Operation 2

Add the first row to the third row:

\[ R_3 \rightarrow R_3 + R_1 \]

Resulting matrix:

\[ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & -1 & -7 \\ 0 & 6 & 1 & 4 \end{array} \right] \]

Row Operation 3

Multiply the second row by -3 and add to the third row:

\[ R_3 \rightarrow R_3 + 3R_2 \]

Resulting matrix:

\[ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & -1 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 & -17 \end{array} \right] \]

Step 3: Back Substitution

Now solve for the variables starting from the last row and working upward.

From the Third Row

\[ -2x_3 = -17 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x_3 = \frac{17}{2} \]

Substitute \( x_3 \) into the Second Row

\[ -2x_2 - \frac{17}{2} = -7 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x_2 = \frac{3}{2} \]

Substitute \( x_2 \) and \( x_3 \) into the First Row

\[ 2x_1 + 3 \left(\frac{3}{2}\right) - \frac{17}{2} = 5 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x_1 = 2 \]

The solution to the system of equations is:

\[ \begin{aligned} x_1 &= 2 \\ x_2 &= \frac{3}{2} \\ x_3 &= \frac{17}{2} \end{aligned} \]
