EXAMPLE

Solve the system of linear equations using Jacobi Method

\[ \begin{align*} 4x_1 - x_2 + x_3 & = 7 \\ -x_1 + 3x_2 - 2x_3 & = -8 \\ x_1 - 2x_2 + 5x_3 & = 6 \end{align*} \]

SOLUTION

The corresponding matrix equation is \( Ax = b \), where:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & -1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & 5 \end{pmatrix}, \quad x = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{pmatrix}, \quad b = \begin{pmatrix} 7 \\ -8 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} \]

Starting with an initial guess \( x^{(0)} = (0, 0, 0) \), the Jacobi iteration will update each component using the formula:

\[ \begin{align*} x_1^{(k+1)} & = \frac{1}{4} \left( 7 + x_2^{(k)} - x_3^{(k)} \right) \\ x_2^{(k+1)} & = \frac{1}{3} \left( -8 + x_1^{(k)} + 2x_3^{(k)} \right) \\ x_3^{(k+1)} & = \frac{1}{5} \left( 6 - x_1^{(k)} + 2x_2^{(k)} \right) \end{align*} \]

By iterating this process, the solution converges to the correct values of \( x_1 \), \( x_2 \), and \( x_3 \).
