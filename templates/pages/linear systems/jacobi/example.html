EXAMPLE

Solve the equation \( x^3 + x - 1 = 0 \) using the fixed-point method starting with an initial guess \( x_0 = 0.5\)


SOLUTION

Step 1: Transform the Equation

Rewrite the equation \( x^3 + x - 1 = 0 \) into the form \( x = g(x) \). One possible transformation is:

\( x = 1 - x^3 \)

So, \( g(x) = 1 - x^3 \).


Step 2: Choose an Initial Guess

Initial guess \( x_0 = 0.5 \).


Step 3: Iterate Using the Fixed-Point Formula

Using the formula \( x_{n+1} = g(x_n) \), we iterate to find successive approximations to the fixed point.


  1. First Iteration:
    \( x_1 = g(x_0) = 1 - (0.5)^3 = 1 - 0.125 = 0.875 \)
  2. Second Iteration:
    \( x_2 = g(x_1) = 1 - (0.875)^3 = 1 - 0.669921875 = 0.330078125 \)
  3. Third Iteration:
    \( x_3 = g(x_2) = 1 - (0.330078125)^3 = 1 - 0.0359375772 = 0.9640624228 \)
  4. Fourth Iteration:
    \( x_4 = g(x_3) = 1 - (0.9640624228)^3 = 1 - 0.8957962642 = 0.1042037358 \)
  5. Fifth Iteration:
    \( x_5 = g(x_4) = 1 - (0.1042037358)^3 = 1 - 0.0011312478 = 0.9988687522 \)

Step 4: Check for Convergence

We continue iterating until the difference between successive approximations is smaller than a chosen tolerance level, indicating convergence. For practical purposes, we will stop here, but in real applications, you might want to continue until the difference between \( x_n \) and \( x_{n+1} \) is sufficiently small.


Conclusion

After a few iterations, we see that the sequence \( x_n \) is converging. The approximations suggest that the fixed point (and hence the root of the original equation) is close to 1.


To confirm, we can continue iterating or use a more precise method to check:

\( x_6 = g(x_5) = 1 - (0.9988687522)^3 = 1 - 0.9966097745 = 0.0033902255 \)

As the iterations progress, \( x_n \) will continue to converge to the true fixed point (root) of the equation \( x = 1 - x^3 \).


This example demonstrates the application of the fixed-point method to find a root of the given equation, illustrating how to transform the equation, choose an initial guess, iterate using the fixed-point formula, and check for convergence.
