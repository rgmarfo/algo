EXERCISE


1. Solve the following system of linear equations using the Jacobi method:

\[ \begin{aligned} 5x_1 - 2x_2 + x_3 &= 1 \\ 3x_1 + 4x_2 - 2x_3 &= 8 \\ -2x_1 + x_2 + 5x_3 &= 2 \end{aligned} \]

Start with an initial guess of \( x^{(0)} = (0, 0, 0) \) and perform two iterations.


2. Consider the following system of linear equations:

\[ \begin{aligned} 6x_1 - x_2 + 2x_3 &= 5 \\ 2x_1 + 7x_2 - x_3 &= 3 \\ 4x_1 - 2x_2 + 8x_3 &= 12 \end{aligned} \]

Analyze whether the Jacobi method will converge for this system. Justify your answer based on the matrix properties.


3. Given the system of equations:

\[ \begin{aligned} 7x_1 + 2x_2 + 3x_3 &= 10 \\ x_1 + 5x_2 + x_3 &= 7 \\ 3x_1 + x_2 + 6x_3 &= 8 \end{aligned} \]

Perform three iterations of the Jacobi method using the initial guess \( x^{(0)} = (1, 1, 1) \). Provide the values of \( x_1^{(3)} \), \( x_2^{(3)} \), and \( x_3^{(3)} \).


4. Consider the system:

\[ \begin{aligned} 3x_1 + x_2 &= 4 \\ x_1 + 2x_2 &= 3 \end{aligned} \]

Apply the Jacobi method with the initial guess \( x^{(0)} = (0, 0) \). Find \( x_1^{(2)} \) and \( x_2^{(2)} \) and discuss the convergence behavior of the method.
