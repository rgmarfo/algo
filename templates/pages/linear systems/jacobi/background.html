BACKGROUND

JACOBI METHOD

The fixed-point method as a numerical technique doesn't have a specific single originator unlike methods named after specific individuals like Newton-Raphson or Euler. Instead, the concept of finding fixed points of functions through iterative methods has been developed and refined over time by various mathematicians and scholars. The method itself draws on fundamental concepts in mathematics, particularly in analysis and numerical analysis, where the idea of fixed points is central. Mathematicians and researchers over centuries have contributed to the understanding and application of fixed-point problems and iterative methods, laying the groundwork for its use in modern numerical techniques. While no single individual is credited with introducing the fixed-point method in its entirety, its principles have been integrated into broader mathematical theory and computational methods through the contributions of many scholars across different periods of mathematical history.
