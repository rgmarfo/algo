BACKGROUND

The Jacobi method is an iterative technique used to solve systems of linear equations. Named after the German mathematician Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi, the method is based on the idea of decomposing a matrix into its diagonal, lower, and upper components. By isolating the diagonal elements, the Jacobi method approximates the solution of the system through successive iterations. Each iteration refines the solution by assuming the values from the previous iteration. The method is particularly useful for large, sparse matrices where direct methods like Gaussian elimination are computationally expensive. However, it requires the matrix to be diagonally dominant or strictly diagonally dominant for guaranteed convergence. Despite its simplicity, the Jacobi method is often less efficient compared to more advanced iterative techniques but remains a foundational approach in numerical linear algebra.
