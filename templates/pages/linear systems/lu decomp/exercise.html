EXERCISE


1. Use the Newton-Raphson method to find the root of the equation \( f(x) = x^3 - 2x - 5 \). Start with an initial guess \( x_0 = 2 \). Perform three iterations and provide the approximate root after each iteration.


2. Apply the Newton-Raphson method to find the root of the equation \( f(x) = e^x - 3x \). Use an initial guess of \( x_0 = 1 \). Perform four iterations and provide the results of each iteration.


3. Determine the root of the equation \( f(x) = \cos(x) - x \) using the Newton-Raphson method. Use \( x_0 = 0.5 \) as the initial guess. Continue the iterations until the approximate root converges to a satisfactory level of accuracy.


4. Find the root of the function \( f(x) = x^4 - x - 10 \) using the Newton-Raphson method with an initial guess \( x_0 = 2 \). Continue the iterations until the approximate root converges to a satisfactory level of accuracy.


5. Use the Newton-Raphson method to find the root of the equation \( f(x) = \sin(x) - 0.5 \). Start with \( x_0 = 0.5 \). Continue the iterations until the approximate root converges to a satisfactory level of accuracy.
