EXAMPLE

Consider the matrix \( A \): \[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] We will perform LU decomposition to factorize \( A \) into the product of a lower triangular matrix \( L \) and an upper triangular matrix \( U \), such that \( A = LU \).

SOLUTION

Step 1: Initialize \( L \) and \( U \) Start with \( L \) as the identity matrix and \( U \) as matrix \( A \): \[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ U = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 2: Perform Row Operations

Row Operation 1: Eliminate the first element in the second and third rows using the first row:
For row 2: \( R_2 \rightarrow R_2 - \frac{3}{4}R_1 \) \[ U_{2,:} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{3}{4} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} \] For row 3: \( R_3 \rightarrow R_3 - \frac{1}{2}R_1 \) \[ U_{3,:} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\frac{1}{2} & 0 \end{bmatrix} \] Update \( L \): \[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{3}{4} & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{1}{2} & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] Row Operation 2: Eliminate the second element in the third row using the second row:
For row 3: \( R_3 \rightarrow R_3 - 2R_2 \) \[ U_{3,:} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\frac{1}{2} & 0 \end{bmatrix} - 2 \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] Update \( L \): \[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{3}{4} & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{1}{2} & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Result

The matrices \( L \) and \( U \) are: \[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{3}{4} & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{1}{2} & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ U = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & -\frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] To verify, compute \( LU \): \[ LU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{3}{4} & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{1}{2} & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & -\frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 & 2 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = A \] Thus, the LU decomposition is correct.
