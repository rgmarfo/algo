EXERCISE


1. Use the forward difference method to approximate the first derivative of the function \( f(t) = t^2 + 3t \) at \( t = 1 \) with a step size \( h = 0.2 \).


2. Apply the forward difference method to approximate the solution of the ordinary differential equation \( \frac{dy}{dt} = 2t - y \) with the initial condition \( y(0) = 3 \), for \( t = 0 \) to \( t = 0.4 \) with a step size \( h = 0.2 \). Find \( y(t) \) at \( t = 0.4 \).


3. Use the forward difference method to approximate the solution of the heat equation \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \) with initial condition \( u(x, 0) = \sin(\pi x) \) and boundary conditions \( u(0, t) = 0 \) and \( u(1, t) = 0 \). Use a spatial step size \( \Delta x = 0.1 \) and a time step size \( \Delta t = 0.01 \). Compute \( u(x, t) \) at \( x = 0.1 \) and \( t = 0.01 \).


4. Apply the forward difference method to approximate the solution of the logistic growth model \( \frac{dy}{dt} = 0.5 y \left(1 - \frac{y}{10}\right) \) with the initial condition \( y(0) = 2 \). Approximate \( y(t) \) for \( t = 0 \) to \( t = 0.5 \) with a step size \( h = 0.1 \).


5. Apply the forward difference method to approximate the solution of the heat equation

\( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \) where \( \alpha = 0.01 \). Given the initial condition \( u(x, 0) = \sin(\pi x) \) and boundary conditions \( u(0, t) = 0 \) and \( u(1, t) = 0 \), use a spatial step size \( \Delta x = 0.1 \) and a time step size \( \Delta t = 0.01 \). Compute \( u(x, t) \) at \( x = 0.1 \) and \( t = 0.01 \).
