EXAMPLE


Use the forward difference method to approximate the solution of the ordinary differential equation

\( \frac{dy}{dt} = y - t \) with the initial condition \( y(0) = 1 \), for \( t = 0 \) to \( t = 0.2 \) with a step size \( h = 0.1 \).


SOLUTION


  1. Forward Difference Method Formula:

    2. The forward difference method for approximating the solution of an ODE is:

    \[ \frac{y_{n+1} - y_n}{h} = f(t_n, y_n) \]

    Rearranging to solve for \( y_{n+1} \):

    \[ y_{n+1} = y_n + h \cdot f(t_n, y_n) \]

  2. Given:

  3. \(\frac{dy}{dt} = y - t\)
  4. Initial condition: \( y(0) = 1 \)
  5. Step size \( h = 0.1 \)
  6. We need to find \( y(t) \) at \( t = 0.2 \).
  7. First Step (from \( t = 0 \) to \( t = 0.1 \)):

    8. At \( t_0 = 0 \), \( y_0 = 1 \).

    Calculate \( y_1 \) using the forward difference method:

    \[ f(t_0, y_0) = y_0 - t_0 = 1 - 0 = 1 \]

    \[ y_1 = y_0 + h \cdot f(t_0, y_0) = 1 + 0.1 \cdot 1 = 1 + 0.1 = 1.1 \]

  8. Second Step (from \( t = 0.1 \) to \( t = 0.2 \)):

    9. At \( t_1 = 0.1 \), \( y_1 = 1.1 \).

    Calculate \( y_2 \) using the forward difference method:

    \[ f(t_1, y_1) = y_1 - t_1 = 1.1 - 0.1 = 1.0 \]

    \[ y_2 = y_1 + h \cdot f(t_1, y_1) = 1.1 + 0.1 \cdot 1.0 = 1.1 + 0.1 = 1.2 \]

  9. Conclusion:

    10. After two steps, the approximate solution at \( t = 0.2 \) is:

    \[ y(0.2) \approx 1.2 \]
