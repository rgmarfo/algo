BACKGROUND

Forward Difference Technique

The forward difference method is a numerical technique used to approximate derivatives of functions. It estimates the derivative of a function \( y \) at a point \( t_n \) using values at \( t_n \) and \( t_{n+1} \). The forward difference approximation for the first derivative is given by: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} \approx \frac{y_{n+1} - y_n}{\Delta t} \] where \( \Delta t \) is the step size. This method is straightforward to implement and computationally efficient, making it suitable for many applications. However, it can be less stable than implicit methods, especially for stiff problems. The forward difference method is commonly used in explicit time-stepping schemes for solving ordinary differential equations and is also applicable in numerical differentiation and finite difference methods for partial differential equations. Its simplicity comes with the trade-off of potential numerical instability and truncation errors.
