BACKWARD DIFFERENCE

False Position (or) Regula Falsi Method

The False Position Method (also known as the Regula Falsi Method) is a root-finding algorithm that combines the bisection method and linear interpolation. It is used to find the root of a function f(x)=0. This method is especially useful for continuous functions where a sign change is observed, indicating the presence of a root. The False Position Method has its roots in ancient mathematics and was known to ancient Egyptians. The method was later formalized in the 16th century and became a common technique for solving equations before the advent of more sophisticated methods. It provided a practical way to approximate solutions of equations when exact solutions were difficult to obtain. The false-position method is a modification on the bisection method. In mathematics, an ancient method of solving an equation in one variable is the false position method or regula falsi method. In simple words, the method is described as the trial and error approach of using “false” or “test” values for the variable and then ltering the test value according to the result.
