EXERCISE


1. Derive the backward difference formula for the first derivative of a function \(f(x)\).


2. Explain the main differences between the forward difference and backward difference methods.


3. Use the backward difference method to approximate the second derivative of \( f(x) = e^x \) at \(x = 1\) with a step size \(h = 0.1\)


4. Use the backward difference method to approximate the solution of the ordinary differential equation \( \frac{dy}{dx} = y - x, \) given the initial condition \( y(0) = 1 \), for \( x = 0 \) to x = 1 with a step size h = 0.1


5. Apply the backward difference method to solve the heat equation \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \) for \(\alpha = 1\), with initial condition \(u(x,0) = \sin(\pi x)\), and boundary conditions \(u(0,t) = 0\) and \(u(1,t) = 0\).
