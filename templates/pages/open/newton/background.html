BACKGROUND

Newton technique

The Newton-Raphson method is named after Sir Isaac Newton and Joseph Raphson. Newton first developed the method in the 17th century, and Raphson later published a simplified version of Newton's method in 1690. It is an iterative technique for finding successively better approximations to the roots (or zeroes) of a real-valued function. This method is particularly powerful and efficient for solving nonlinear equations, and it has a wide range of applications in mathematics, engineering, and applied sciences. The goal of the Newton-Raphson method is to find a solution \( x \) such that \( f(x) = 0 \). The method starts with an initial guess \( x_0 \) and generates a sequence of improving approximations to the root. The idea is to use the tangent line at a point on the function to estimate the next point closer to the root.
