Open Methods Details

Open Methods are numerical techniques used to find roots of equations by iteratively updating an initial guess. Unlike bracketing methods, open methods do not require an initial interval containing the root. These methods can converge faster but may not always converge or may converge to a wrong root if not applied carefully.

Steps of an Open Method

  1. Choose an initial guess \( x_0 \).
  2. Iteratively update the guess using a function to obtain a sequence of approximations \( x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n \).
  3. Repeat the process until a satisfactory approximation is obtained.

Newton-Raphson Method

Find the root of \( f(x) = x^2 - 4 \).

1. Initial guess: \( x_0 = 2 \)

2. Update formula: \( x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} \)

3. Derivative: \( f'(x) = 2x \)

4. Iteration 1: \( x_1 = 2 - \frac{2^2 - 4}{2 \times 2} = 1.5 \)

5. Continue iterations until convergence.

Secant Method

Find the root of \( f(x) = x^2 - 4 \).

1. Initial guesses: \( x_0 = 1.5 \) and \( x_1 = 2 \)

2. Update formula: \( x_{n+1} = x_n - f(x_n) \times \frac{x_n - x_{n-1}}{f(x_n) - f(x_{n-1})} \)

3. Iteration 2: Calculate \( x_2 \) using the formula.

4. Continue iterations until convergence.
