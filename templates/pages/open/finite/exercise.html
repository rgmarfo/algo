EXERCISE


1. Consider the 1D Poisson equation \( u''(x) = 5 \) on the interval \([0, 2]\) with boundary conditions \(u(0) = 0\) and \(u(2) = 1\). Use the finite difference method to set up the equations.


2. Consider the 1D Poisson equation \( u''(x) = -4 \) on the interval \([0, 1]\) with boundary conditions \(u(0) = 0\) and \(u(1) = 1\). Use the finite difference method to set up the equations.


3. Consider the 1D heat equation \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \) on the interval \([0, 1]\) with initial condition \(u(x, 0) = x(1-x)\) and boundary conditions \(u(0, t) = 0\) and \(u(1, t) = 0\). Use the finite difference method to derive the discretized equations.


4. Consider the 1D heat equation \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \) on the interval \([0, 1]\) with initial condition \(u(x, 0) = x(1-x)\) and boundary conditions \(u(0, t) = 0\) and \(u(1, t) = 0\). Use the finite difference method to derive the discretized equations.


5. Consider the 1D Burgers' equation \( u_t + u u_x = \nu u_{xx} \) on the interval \([0, 1]\) with initial condition \(u(x, 0) = \sin(\pi x)\) and boundary conditions \(u(0, t) = 0\) and \(u(1, t) = 0\). Use the finite difference method to set up the equations.
