EXERCISE


1. Use the forward difference method to approximate the first derivative of \( f(x) = x^2 + 3x + 2 \) at \( x = 1 \) with a step size \( h = 0.1 \).



2. Apply the backward difference method to estimate the first derivative of \( f(x) = e^x \) at \( x = 2 \) with a step size \( h = 0.05 \).



3. Using the central difference method, calculate the second derivative of \( f(x) = \ln(x) \) at \( x = 3 \) with a step size \( h = 0.1 \).



4. Estimate the first derivative of \( f(x) = \sin(x) \) at \( x = \frac{\pi}{6} \) using the central difference method with a step size \( h = 0.01 \).



5. For the function \( f(x) = x^3 - 2x + 1 \), approximate the second derivative at \( x = 1 \) using the forward difference method with a step size \( h = 0.2 \).
