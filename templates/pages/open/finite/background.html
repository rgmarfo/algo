BACKGROUND

FINITE DIFFERENCE METHOD

The Finite Difference Method (FDM) is a numerical technique used to solve differential equations by approximating derivatives with finite difference equations. It is widely used in various fields of science and engineering, including physics, chemistry, biology, finance, and engineering, to solve problems that involve differential equations. The origins of the Finite Difference Method can be traced back to the early 18th century. The development of FDM is closely linked to the progress in calculus and numerical analysis made by early mathematicians. Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, in the late 17th century, laid the foundation for the numerical approximation of derivatives through their development of calculus. Their work on differentiation and integration provided the essential tools for the approximation methods that would later form the basis of FDM. In the finite difference method, the derivatives in the differential equation are approximated using the finite difference formulas.
